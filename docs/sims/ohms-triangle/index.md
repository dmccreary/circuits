## How to Use

**Click any coloured section of the triangle** to choose which quantity to solve for — that section goes dark with a `?`.

| Section clicked | Solves for | Formula used |
|-----------------|-----------|--------------|
| Top (amber) — V | Voltage | \(V = I \times R\) |
| Bottom-left (blue) — I | Current | \(I = V / R\) |
| Bottom-right (green) — R | Resistance | \(R = V / I\) |

**Drag the two sliders** at the bottom to set the known values.  The result and power (**P**) update immediately in the calculator panel on the right.

## Ohm's Law

\[V = I \times R\]

| Symbol | Quantity | SI Unit |
|--------|----------|---------|
| V | Voltage (potential difference) | Volt (V) |
| I | Current | Ampere (A) |
| R | Resistance | Ohm (Ω) |

Rearranged:

\[I = \frac{V}{R} \qquad R = \frac{V}{I}\]

## Power

Power is always shown as a bonus calculation:

\[P = V \cdot I = I^2 R = \frac{V^2}{R}\]

| Unit | Equivalents |
|------|-------------|
| 1 W = 1 V·A | 1 mW = 0.001 W |

## The Triangle Mnemonic

Cover the quantity you want to find — the remaining two show what to do:

- Cover **V** → multiply the other two: \(I \times R\)
- Cover **I** → divide top by bottom-right: \(V / R\)
- Cover **R** → divide top by bottom-left: \(V / I\)
